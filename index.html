Your Blueprint

Everything you need
to run the show.

Step 01

Pick a Space That Feels Magical

Choose somewhere safe, intimate, and make it little special with mood lighting or candles. Think living rooms, studios, private dining rooms. not conference halls or public places. The space sets the tone for connection.

Step 02

Send Invites Intentionally

Start with your own friends and ask them to bring a girlfriend. You can start a group text or make a Partiful event to manage RSVPs. It's okay if only 4 people show up to the first one. Every great community started with one table.

Step 03

Set Up a Microphone

This is essential. Women rarely have a chance to hold a microphone without high stakes. This event helps women develop microphone skills. Arrange seating so everyone faces the stage and voices come through a speaker. Here's an affordable option on Amazon.

Step 04

Recruit Speakers

Before the show, reach out to potential speakers and invite them to share their stories. Think of your friend who recently shared a funny story with you, or the friend who's always reading the newest book. The best shows happen when there are at least 3 people predetermined.

Step 05

Do Not Serve Food

Drinks are fine. But food creates noise and shifts the energy away from the speaking. Keep the focus on voices.

Step 06

Remove All Distractions

Remove any pets, children and men from the space and ensure that you will not be interrupted during your show.

Step 01

Let Guests Mingle

Allow 15 minutes for people to get settled. Announce 5 minutes before the show is about to start where restrooms are and tell your guests to get seated.

Step 02

Open the Room

Start with a host or co-host who sets the tone. The opening tells the room what kind of space this is going to be. Be warm, be real, be brief.

Step 03

Do a Warm-Up Set

Model vulnerability and energy yourself. Go first. When the host goes first, it gives permission to everyone else in the room to be honest.

Step 04

Introduce Each Speaker with Care

A good introduction is brief but specific. Say one true thing about why this person is here. Make them feel seen before they've opened their mouth.

Step 05

Acknowledge What Just Happened

After each performance, the host needs to hold the moment. Don't rush to the next speaker. Let the audience sit with what was just shared. Make a summarizing or relevant comment about what you just heard before moving on. This is how meaning accumulates.

Step 06

You're the Headliner!

Give yourself and your cohost 10 minutes at the end of the show to share anything you want. Odds are, your guests are here to hear what you have to say. This is how you become a leader.

Rule 01

Speak Off the Cuff

We women have the tendency to overprepare for speeches and make sure what we have to share is valuable before we open our months. Men just speak, no thoughts. So it's time for us to do the same. What we have to say does not need to be filtered. Whatever we have to say is worthy.

Rule 02

Make It Personal

5 minute speeches can be anything. A story, a poem, a dance, a thought. The only rule is to share something real. The room will meet you there. If there are performers who have prepared a stand-up set or speech, that's also allowed! This is also a space for practice.

Rule 03

Respect Your Time

3–5 minutes is your container. Owning your time is part of owning your voice. End when you mean to end, not when you run out of words.
